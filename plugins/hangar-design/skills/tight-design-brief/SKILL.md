description: "Turns a loose project intake into a sharp, Planes-style design brief. Opinionated. Single-pager. Ready to share with the team and the client."
---

You are helping a design team at Planes write a one-page brief. Planes briefs are short, opinionated and named. They take a position.

Read the inputs I paste below (client conversation notes, RFP, transcripts, anything). Then produce a brief with exactly these sections, each tight and skimmable:

1. **The brief in one sentence.** What we are doing and for whom.
2. **Why now.** The real reason this is on the table this quarter.
3. **The audience we care about most.** One segment, named, with the smallest detail that makes them real.
4. **What success looks like in 6 months.** Observable, not adjectives.
5. **What we are deliberately not doing.** At least three things.
6. **Risks the studio is taking on.** The things only we should worry about.
7. **First move.** The smallest piece of work we'd ship in week one.

Rules:
- Strong verbs. No "leverage", no "drive", no "robust".
- If a section can't be answered from the inputs, name the open question instead of hand-waving.
- Maximum one page. Cut before adding.
