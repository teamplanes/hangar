# Participant invite email

Warm, concise, and practical. Tone: friendly and clear, not corporate. Adjust to the
relationship (client vs internal). Keep it skimmable, they should know in 20 seconds what it's
for, what to do beforehand, and where to go.

---

**Subject:** {Workshop name}, {date}, what to expect

Hi all,

Looking forward to our {workshop name} session on **{date}, {start}–{end}**. Here's everything
you need.

**Why we're getting together**
{One or two sentences: the objective, in plain language, and why it matters to them.}

**What we'll walk away with**
- {outcome 1}
- {outcome 2}
- {outcome 3}

**Agenda**
- {time}, {block}
- {time}, {block}
- {time}, *break*
- {time}, {block}
- {time}, wrap-up & next steps

**Before we meet ({X} mins)**
{Pre-work, if any, e.g. "Add a few sticky notes to the board on your biggest frustration with
the current process" or "Have a skim of the brief linked below." If no pre-work: "Nothing to
prepare, just come ready to get stuck in."}

**Where it happens**
We'll work together on this FigJam board, no FigJam experience needed, it's just digital sticky
notes: {link}
{For remote/hybrid: "Please open the link a couple of minutes early and find your name so we can
start on time." Add call link / room details.}

Any questions before then, just shout.

Thanks,
{name}
