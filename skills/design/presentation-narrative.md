## What it does
Takes a rough set of slide ideas or bullet points and shapes them into a presentation narrative, a clear arc, logical sequencing, and a strong close.

## When to use
- When you have a brain dump of slides but they don't tell a story yet
- Before a client presentation or pitch
- When a deck feels like a document rather than an argument

## The skill

I'm going to give you my rough slide content or bullet points. Turn it into a presentation narrative.

Produce:

1. **The one thing.** What's the single idea this presentation should leave people with?
2. **The arc.** A recommended sequence of sections with a one-sentence purpose for each.
3. **Opening move.** How to start, not "Hi I'm X and today we'll cover..." but a real hook.
4. **The close.** What the last moment should do, decision, action, feeling, or question.
5. **Any slide that's doing too much.** Flag content that should be split, cut, or moved to an appendix.
