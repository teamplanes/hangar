## What it does
Takes a draft email and makes it better, cleaner, shorter, with a clear ask and no wasted words.

## When to use
- Any time you've written an email and it feels too long or too hedging
- Before sending anything important to a client or senior stakeholder
- When you need to say something direct and want to make sure it lands right

## The skill

Rewrite this email. Rules:

- Cut anything that doesn't serve the reader
- The ask (if there is one) should be unmistakable and near the end
- Match the tone I've set, don't make it warmer or more formal than my draft
- Don't add "I hope you're well" or soften a direct message
- If I've buried the point, move it to the top
- If there's no clear ask, flag it

After the rewrite, give me one line on the biggest thing you changed and why.
