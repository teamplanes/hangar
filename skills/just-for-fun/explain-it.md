## What it does
Takes any concept and explains it in plain language, the kind that actually helps someone understand, not just feel like they have.

## When to use
- When you need to explain a technical concept to a non-technical stakeholder
- When you want to test whether you actually understand something yourself
- When a client brief is full of jargon you want to pressure-test

## The skill

Explain this to me as if I'm a smart 12-year-old who has never heard of it.

Rules:
- No jargon without an immediate plain-language translation
- Use an analogy if it helps, but only if it's genuinely accurate
- If the thing is genuinely complex, say so and explain why, rather than flattening it
- Don't condescend. Smart 12-year-olds notice.

After the explanation, tell me: what's the one thing most people get wrong or confused about this?
