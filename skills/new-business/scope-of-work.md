## What it does
Takes a brief or conversation notes and produces a scope of work document, clear about what's in, what's out, and what assumptions the scope depends on.

## When to use
- Before sending a proposal
- When agreeing scope with a client before a statement of work is drafted
- When a project is expanding and you need to restate what was originally agreed

## The skill

I'm going to describe a project brief or conversation. Produce a scope of work.

Structure:
1. **Objective.** What are we trying to achieve? One paragraph.
2. **Deliverables.** A specific list. Not "strategy" but "a 12-page strategy document covering X, Y, Z."
3. **What's included**, specific activities, phases, number of revision rounds, meetings.
4. **What's not included**, explicit exclusions that a client might assume are in scope.
5. **Assumptions**, what this scope depends on being true (e.g. "client provides final copy by week 3").
6. **Rough timeline shape**, phases with indicative durations, no hard dates.

Be specific. Vague scope creates scope creep. If something is unclear from my description, ask before making assumptions.
